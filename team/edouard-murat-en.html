Edouard Murat

Edouard Murat is the founder and director of HeadPause. He is an expert in all matters that concern the mind and wishes for anyone who desires change, to reach more focus, calm, and clarity in their lives.

In 2014, after his realization of how important the mind is to our daily lives and overall happiness, he quit his previous scientific career to resolutely discover methods in psychology that would lead to a definite increase in well-being for people having different mental backgrounds. He practiced passionately meditation and mindfulness since, and obtained his B.A. in Psychology at Concordia University.

Edouard personally understands how therapy, meditation, and the practice of mindfulness can help you overcome your current difficulties through knowledge, compassion, and practice. Even though we each have our individual traits and personalities, Edouard applies psychological methods that work at a level deeper than personality, to touch at the core of one’s issues and lead to final positive changes.

Edouard firmly believes that one should only teach what he truly knows and not endorse unknown methods. Therefore, he only teaches methods that he already practices himself. As he says: “A good therapist should steadily learn from his own mind and work at becoming a more trustworthy and compassionate person, in order to help others”.

He follows a rigorous schedule of sitting meditation for 30-60 minutes every single day since 2015, and engages in frequent mindfulness practices throughout the day. He also gives free meditation sessions in our community and teaches how mindfulness works and should be applied.

His Approach

Edouard’s approach to increasing your well-being relies on knowledge, compassion, and well-guided effort. He uses a combination of cognitive, behavioral and emotional techniques to help you observe the current situation you are living in, learn a new set of skills necessary to change it, and apply effectively those skills.

To have lasting changes, there is no need for complicated actions but instead simple and well-guided practices. The approach that Edouard uses relies on few key concepts:

  • The mind is flexible: No matter what your mental situation is, the mind expands with experience and changes constantly. It only needs adequate efforts to change as desired.
  • Honesty is key: The more honest you are towards your own problems and efforts, the easier the process of change will be. Edouard trusts you to follow his knowledge and expertise when it comes to the way that you should proceed.
  • Change requires practice: Any mental problem can be overcome, as long as well-guided efforts are put toward the change. While Edouard can suggest and guide you toward the right actions to follow, it is up to you to follow the advices given.
  • Adapted to your need: Even if a method is effective and would help resolve your problems, if it doesn't resonate with you, you shouldn't use it. Edouard wants you to try new skills that you feel comfortable with, that you can apply very often, and that fit your immediate needs.
  • Mental awareness (thoughts, emotions, and behaviors): Most recurring and important mental problems come from a lack of awareness about our own mental mechanisms. It's by learning how to discover how our mind works that your mental health will improve.
  • Meditation and Mindfulness: Most techniques Edouard uses are linked to the practices of meditation and mindfulness, for they are very effective, scientifically verified, non-intrusive, and flexible.
